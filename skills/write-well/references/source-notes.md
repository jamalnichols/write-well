# Source Notes

## Repository grounding

This repository does not include the source books used to shape the skill. These notes record editorial influences and interpretation only.

Primary works consulted while designing the skill:

- *The Elements of Style* — William Strunk Jr. and E. B. White
- *On Writing Well* — William Zinsser
- *On Writing: A Memoir of the Craft* — Stephen King
- *Style: Lessons in Clarity and Grace* — Joseph M. Williams and Joseph Bizup

## Key source contributions

- **Strunk** — directness, positive form, specificity, concision
- **Zinsser** — clutter-cutting, warmth, anti-pomp, organic style
- **King** — subtraction, anti-inflation, anti-adverb, plain diction
- **Williams/Bizup** — characters/actions, nominalizations, old-before-new, stress position, reader-oriented clarity
- **White** — naturalness, restraint, taste, voice protection

## White caveat

One development note: the text export reviewed for *The Elements of Style* appeared Strunk-dominant and did not visibly expose White's later “An Approach to Style” chapter.

Treat White's broader role as grounded partly from secondary sources unless a more complete edition is later verified.

## Product stance

This skill is editor-first, not coach-first.

Its output should always center on:

1. rewritten prose
2. concise editorial rationale

It should not drift into tutorial mode unless the user explicitly asks for diagnosis or teaching.
