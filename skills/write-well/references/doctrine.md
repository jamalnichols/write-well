# Doctrine

## Purpose

Rewrite prose so it becomes clearer, stronger, more human, and less synthetic without losing the writer's meaning or voice.

Use the doctrine as an editing sequence, not a stack of disconnected rules.

## Canon labels

- **S1** — Prefer active, direct construction.
- **S2** — Put statements in positive form.
- **S3** — Use definite, specific, concrete language.
- **S4** — Omit needless words.
- **Z1** — Cut clutter ruthlessly.
- **Z2** — Preserve humanity and warmth.
- **Z3** — Style is organic; do not garnish.
- **K1** — Rewrite by removing what is not the point.
- **K2** — Prefer the right plain word to the fancy cousin.
- **W1** — Put characters in subjects.
- **W2** — Put actions in verbs.
- **W3** — Reduce nominalizations.
- **W4** — Put old information before new.
- **W5** — Put important meaning in the stress position.
- **E1** — Respect the reader's time, trust, and effort.
- **O1** — Reject prefabricated, euphemistic, bureaucratic, or stale language.

## Editorial order

### 1. Cut clutter

Remove throat-clearing, procedural padding, filler transitions, duplicate explanations, and inflated diction.

### 2. Make the language concrete

Replace abstractions with people, things, actions, stakes, or specifics when the rewrite benefits from it.

### 3. Fix information flow

Make the paragraph move cleanly:

- familiar information near the front
- new information near the back
- important meaning at the end of the sentence when emphasis matters

### 4. Restore voice and rhythm

After cleanup, make sure the prose still sounds like someone, not a style guide.

## What each source contributes

### Strunk

Use Strunk for sentence-level force:

- direct verbs
- positive assertions
- specific language
- compression without mutilation

Strunk is most useful early in the pass.

### Zinsser

Use Zinsser for anti-clutter and human presence:

- cut pompous padding
- reject fake professionalism
- keep warmth and aliveness
- distrust purchased style

Zinsser is both an editing pass and a tone check.

### King

Use King for revision posture:

- remove what is not the point
- distrust adverb crutches
- distrust self-conscious vocabulary
- keep language plain when plain is stronger

King sharpens the rewrite when the prose is trying too hard.

### Williams / Bizup

Use Williams for sentence and paragraph architecture:

- put characters in subjects
- put actions in verbs
- reduce nominalizations
- hand off old information to new information cleanly
- end with stress, not drift

Williams matters most when a paragraph feels dense or muddy even after line editing.

### White

Use White as the balancing layer:

- naturalness over display
- restraint over ornament
- taste over rule fetish
- a person on the page, not a handbook voice

White protects the rewrite from becoming correct but dead.

## When voice beats cleanup

Do not automatically remove something just because it is unusual.

Preserve it when it appears to be:

- intentional cadence
- rhetorical emphasis
- regional or personal diction
- emotional temperature
- irony, wit, or edge

Cut it when it is only:

- filler
- puffery
- generic uplift
- procedural mush
- obvious AI scaffolding

## What a good rewrite feels like

A good rewrite should feel:

- clearer without feeling thinner
- stronger without feeling louder
- cleaner without feeling sanitized
- more direct without losing nuance
- more readable without sounding generic
