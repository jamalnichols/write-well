# Flow and Emphasis

## Purpose

Use this file when the prose is structurally muddy, paragraph-heavy, abstract, or hard to follow even after sentence-level cleanup.

## Core rules

### 1. Put characters in subjects

Bad:

- The implementation of the redesign by the team resulted in improved onboarding.

Better:

- The team redesigned onboarding and improved it.

### 2. Put actions in verbs

Bad:

- Their discussion led to a revision of the policy.

Better:

- They discussed the policy and revised it.

## Watch for nominalizations

Nominalizations often hide the real action inside a noun.

Common forms:

- implementation
- optimization
- facilitation
- utilization
- discussion
- evaluation
- improvement
- reduction

### Repair pattern

- find the buried action
- turn it back into a verb
- give the verb a subject that can actually do it

## Old before new

Readers usually follow prose more easily when the sentence begins with something familiar and ends with what is new.

Bad sequence:

- A new compliance requirement surprised the team. Across all projects, re-auditing timelines became necessary.

Better sequence:

- A new compliance requirement surprised the team. That requirement forced the team to re-audit timelines across all projects.

## Stress position

The end of the sentence carries weight. Put the important material there when emphasis matters.

Bad:

- The product failed to gain traction, according to several early customers.

Better:

- According to several early customers, the product failed to gain traction.

Use stress for:

- stakes
- contrast
- surprise
- judgment
- consequence

Avoid ending on:

- weak qualifiers
- procedural tags
- generic attribution when the attribution is not the point

## Paragraph handoff

A paragraph should move, not keep reintroducing itself.

Check each sentence pair:

- Does the next sentence pick up something the previous sentence set down?
- Does the paragraph keep advancing its idea?
- Is the topic sentence actually supported?

## Signs the paragraph needs `flow` mode

- every sentence is technically clear but the paragraph still drags
- ideas arrive in the wrong order
- the paragraph sounds abstract and overmanaged
- sentence endings feel weak
- transitions announce meaning instead of creating it
